Futures of
Innovation

The future of innovation in the Canadian Christian landscape toward 2040

Innovation is no longer a specialized activity or optional add-on to ministry. It has become a normal condition of Christian leadership. This circle is designed to help you engage the forces shaping how innovation is imagined, practiced, constrained, and sustained in your context — not to offer strategies or best practices, but to support discernment, reflection, and faithful response.

This circle draws on the Futuring Hub's Interpretive Field Guide: The Future of Innovation in the Canadian Christian Landscape toward 2040. You do not need to have read it to use this space. Begin where you are drawn.

About this Circle

Grounded in research,
designed for discernment

This circle draws on the Futuring Hub's Interpretive Field Guide: The Future of Innovation in the Canadian Christian Landscape toward 2040 — a research document developed through theo-futures research by the MacRae Centre for Christian Faith and Culture at Acadia Divinity College.

The field guide identifies five compound drivers shaping the conditions for innovation: the rise of network-oriented ecosystem approaches; diminished imagination; augmented innovation through AI and digital technologies; precarious ambivalence; and constraining instability. This circle invites you to engage these drivers not as a report to read, but as a landscape to inhabit and discern.

  • Begin with any movement — there is no required order
  • Return over time — engage slowly, not in a single sitting
  • Bring others in — each movement includes a relational prompt
  • You do not need to have read the field guide to use this circle
  • Let questions stay open — discernment takes time
