01 Encounter

Someone who completed this circle.
What they're carrying now.

This is not a success story. It is a reflection — offered honestly, without resolution. Read it slowly.

"I came into this circle hoping for clarity about what to do next. I didn't get that — at least not the kind I was looking for. What I got instead was a clearer picture of why I'd been so reluctant to name what I was actually seeing. I'd been calling it wisdom. It was mostly fear.

The movement that stayed with me longest was Discern. Not the tools — the question about who wasn't in the room. I realized I'd been making decisions in a circle that was comfortable and mostly agreed with me, and calling that leadership. I haven't fixed that yet. But I've stopped pretending it isn't true.

I don't know if my community will innovate. I'm not even sure that's the right frame anymore. I know I'm paying attention differently — to things I was letting pass without naming, to voices I was politely including but not actually hearing. That feels like something. I hope it's enough."
Ministry leader, Atlantic Canada
Completed the Futures of Innovation circle — twelve months ago

03 Reframe

Three categories for what you're carrying.

Learning that is worth keeping takes different forms. Select the category that feels most alive for you right now — the one that most honestly describes the shape of your learning from this circle.

Something Has Shifted

The learning has changed something real — in how you see, how you decide, or how you lead. It may be small. It is not theoretical. It is showing up in behavior, even if only beginning to.

What would be different in a meeting, a decision, or a conversation this week if this shift were integrated?
Something Remains Unresolved

The learning has opened a question you can't close yet. That's not a failure — it may be the most honest form of real learning. You're carrying something you can't quite put down.

Is this a question you can sit with for a season — or one that requires a conversation, a decision, or an act before it can be carried productively?
Something Worth Carrying Forward

The learning hasn't resolved into action yet, but it has named something — a new frame, a renewed commitment, a question that is worth inhabiting over the long term. You know it matters. You're not sure how yet.

Who needs to know that you're carrying this? And what would it mean to share it — not as a conclusion, but as an ongoing question?

Challenge

The Gap Between Insight and Change

Learning that changes nothing is not yet formation. Formation shows up in behavior, relationship, and decision over time — not in what we can articulate, but in what we actually do.

"You may have learned something here. But have you changed anything? What would be different — in a meeting, a decision, a conversation, a practice — if this learning were genuinely integrated into how you lead? Name one specific thing. Not a posture. A behavior."

For teams and leadership groups — Shared Integration Circle +
  1. Gather the group. Open with two minutes of quiet.
  2. Each person shares their three sentences. No interruption, no debate — just listening.
  3. After all have shared, name together: What are we carrying collectively that we didn't have before? Where do our sentences overlap?
  4. Ask: What do we need to release — an assumption, a practice, a priority — to make room for what we're learning?
  5. Close by identifying one concrete, shared change you will make in the next ninety days that reflects what this circle has opened.
05 Integrate

What is this circle forming you to become?

A circle like this one does not complete formation. At best it names what formation requires, interrupts what would have continued unchanged, and points toward the kind of ongoing practice that makes it possible. That is not nothing. But it is a beginning, not an arrival. Name what you're carrying forward.
