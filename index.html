Formation spaces for integration, discernment, and faithful response

A Leadership Circle is a digital formation experience designed to help you integrate learning from a specific event, research initiative, or shared encounter. It is not a course to complete. It is a space to inhabit — at your own pace, with others, over time.

Each circle is organized around six sensemaking movements: postures that help you attend, interpret, discern, imagine, name your readiness, and learn. Choose a circle below to begin.

What is a Leadership Circle?

A formation
practice, not
a program

A Leadership Circle is a digital space designed for slow, reflective engagement with a specific theme or research initiative. It is structured around six sensemaking movements — Attend, Interpret, Discern, Imagine, Name Readiness, and Learn — that function as postures rather than steps.

You can begin anywhere, return over time, and engage at whatever pace makes sense for you and your context. Circles are designed to be used individually, in pairs, or in small groups.

  • Begin with any movement — there is no required starting point
  • Return over time — each movement is designed for slow engagement
  • Use with others — relational prompts are built into every movement
  • Let questions stay open — you are not expected to arrive at answers
