Age of
Instability

Navigating the convergence of forces reshaping Christian leadership and ministry

We are living through a period of compounding, converging disruptions. Technological acceleration, geopolitical realignment, ecological crisis, economic volatility, and information breakdown are not separate problems — they are interlocking forces reshaping what ministry is, what community means, and what faithful leadership requires. This circle is designed to help you attend to these forces honestly, interpret them theologically, and discern what they demand of you and your community.

This circle draws on the Futuring Hub's Navigating the Future: Age of Instability research report (June 2025). You do not need to have read the report to use this space. Begin where you are drawn.

About this Circle

Grounded in research,
designed for discernment

This circle draws on the Futuring Hub's Navigating the Future: Age of Instability — a research report produced by the MacRae Centre for Christian Faith and Culture at Acadia Divinity College in June 2025.

The report identifies six interlocking macro forces — technological advancement and information crisis, geopolitical realignment and political volatility, polycrisis and complexity, ecological crisis, economic instability, and universal accelerating change — and traces their implications for Christian communities and leaders. This circle invites you to engage these forces not as a report to analyze, but as a landscape to inhabit, pray through, and discern together.

  • Begin with any movement — there is no required order
  • Return over time — engage slowly, not in a single sitting
  • Bring others in — each movement includes a group practice
  • You do not need to have read the report to use this circle
  • Instability is the context, not the enemy — let the circle change how you see it
