Self-Curated
Spirituality

Navigating the shift from inherited faith to personalized spiritual practice

Across Canadian society, the relationship between individuals and religious institutions is being fundamentally renegotiated. People are not simply leaving faith — they are reshaping it on their own terms: blending traditions, curating practices, following digital teachers, and constructing spiritual lives that feel authentic to them but may have little connection to any inherited community or doctrine. This circle is designed to help Christian leaders attend honestly to this shift, interpret it theologically, and discern what faithful response actually requires.

This circle draws on the Futuring Hub's April 2025 report on Self-Curated Spirituality. You do not need to have read the report to engage this space. Begin where you are drawn.

About this Circle

Grounded in research,
designed for discernment

This circle draws on the Futuring Hub's Self-Curated Spirituality — a research report produced by the MacRae Centre for Christian Faith and Culture at Acadia Divinity College, April 2025.

The report traces how post-modernity, hyper-individualism, digital culture, and declining institutional trust have combined to produce a new spiritual landscape — one in which individuals construct personal faith pathways from multiple sources, often outside any single tradition or community. The circle invites leaders not to treat this as a problem to solve, but as a landscape to inhabit honestly, interpret theologically, and respond to with imagination and depth.

  • Begin with any movement — there is no required order
  • Return over time — engage slowly, not in a single sitting
  • Bring others in — each movement includes a group practice
  • You do not need to have read the report to use this circle
  • The goal is not to defend the church — it is to discern what faithfulness requires
